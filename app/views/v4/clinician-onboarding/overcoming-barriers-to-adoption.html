Overcoming barriers to adoption

See also the section What good looks like and case studies for more examples of best practice.

Assessing patients' digital skills

By making an assessment of a patient's digital skills part of routine clinical contact, practitioners can increase the likelihood of the care being well matched to the capabilities of the patient.

At the Roxton Practice in North Lincolnshire they implemented a formal scoring system of patient's digital capabilities.

This was then incorporated into pathways which would define what type of communications each patient would receive.

Using in person help to support adoption

Systematic and targeted in person help can help people onboard onto apps. This can be provided by people with limited clinical training.

Supporting users to get onto the NHS App

Research shows that whilst many people may struggle to install an app, if they are helped to do so, once it is installed, they will use it.

Some clinical settings have had success in using non-clinical 'digital navigators' to help onboard people.

There are a number of resources available to help people install the NHS App:

  • NHS App step by step guide
  • Setting up the NHS App

Working with local partners and third sector organisations

Many practices who have had success in helping people onboard onto using the NHS App and digital therapeutics have worked with other organisations within their neighbourhood or local area.

Taking a multi-agency approach can also help address complex health and care needs.
