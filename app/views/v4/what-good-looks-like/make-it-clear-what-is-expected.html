Make it clear what is expected of patients and keep it simple

Using digital therapeutics for remote monitoring

In West Yorkshire, patients spoke warmly about MyCare24, a COPD remote monitoring service that ran from Airedale Hospital in 2021, with patients using the Luscii app, with remote monitoring via a 'virtual hospital' model.

Each day, patients entered two readings (pulse and oxygen level, using an oximeter), a fairly easy and quick task. If someone's readings were not normal, the virtual team was alerted and a nurse would be allocated to call for help. As one nurse put it, a patient can enter a reading at 3am and know someone will call if there is a concern.

The results were striking. For 232 patients, comparing the six months before and after they joined, COPD-related hospital days fell by 63% and emergency admissions by 29%.
