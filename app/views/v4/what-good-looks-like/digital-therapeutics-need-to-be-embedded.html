Digital therapeutics need to be embedded into a service or pathway

From research we know that digital therapeutics are at their most successful when embedded into a clinical pathway. Patients are referred into a clinical service, which just happens to have an app as part of it.

They fail when patients are handed a tool with no support around them. Unsupported rollout also has the danger of widening health inequalities. Those with the greatest need often need the most support.

"We refer to a service from general practice. We don't even talk about the technology so much. The therapeutic is an option within an established pathway."

"Bad deployment of technology exacerbates health inequalities. When you go fully digital – the danger is people just vanish. We saw that in COVID."

Dr James Cowling, Roxton Practice, North East Lincolnshire

At Roxton, a digital therapeutic became part of the COPD pathway, led by one named respiratory consultant who keeps it safe. Other clinicians refer in, so no one carries the risk alone. The team also built support for patients with low digital confidence.
