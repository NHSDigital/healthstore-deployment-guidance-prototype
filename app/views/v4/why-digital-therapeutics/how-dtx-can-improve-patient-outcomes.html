How digital therapeutics can improve patient outcomes

Digital therapeutics can improve patient outcomes in several ways.

Support behaviour change over time

Sustained behaviour change around exercise, diet, medication adherence and symptom monitoring is difficult to achieve in short clinical encounters. Digital therapeutics create the daily touchpoints that support lasting change.

Build confidence to self-manage

Patients uncertain about what is safe to do often disengage from recovery. A digital therapeutic provides structure and reassurance that builds confidence to act independently. This can help reduce anxiety-driven contacts and unnecessary appointments.

Extend the reach of a clinical programme

For patients who cannot access in-person services, due to geography, work or caring responsibilities, a digital therapeutic may be the only structured support available. For patients who have completed a clinical programme, it provides a follow-on that preserves the gains made.

Reduce pressure on services

Evidence from deployed digital therapeutics shows reductions in unplanned GP contacts, emergency department attendances and hospital readmissions for patients who engage with structured digital support.
