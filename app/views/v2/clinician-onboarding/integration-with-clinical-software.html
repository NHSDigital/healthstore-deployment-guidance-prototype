Integration with clinical software

Prescribing via DM+D

Conceptually the recommendation of a digital therapeutic is treated like a prescription, as part of a care plan and clinical pathway. It should always be something which is talked through with the patient prior to prescription.

The practical means of triggering the digital therapeutic onboarding journey is the electronic prescription service, accessed via the DM+D dropdown in electronic patient records software.

Communications via NHS App and onboarding journey

Once you have prescribed a patient a digital therapeutic, the NHS HealthStore service will trigger NHS Notify to send the patient a message via the NHS App.

They will then be prompted to download the relevant digital therapeutic and log on to it using their NHS Login identity.

If they do not successfully onboard onto the app within 7 days after the initial prompt they will receive an SMS reminder.

The prescription remains valid for 12 months after the initial prescription.

If they do not have the NHS app installed

Patients who do not have the NHS app installed will receive an SMS message. They may need help installing the app and getting P9 verification.

There are a number of resources available to help people install the NHS App:

  • NHS App step by step guide
  • Setting up the NHS App

Research shows that whilst many people may struggle to install an app, if they are helped to do so, they are sufficiently skilled to use it.

Some clinical settings have had success in using non-clinical 'digital navigators' to help onboard people.

Patients who do not use smartphones

Some digital therapeutics can be used via desktop or laptop-based browsers.

The NHS App can also be accessed via desktop or laptop-based browsers. For patients who prefer to access this way then this route is possible.

They can be notified via SMS.
