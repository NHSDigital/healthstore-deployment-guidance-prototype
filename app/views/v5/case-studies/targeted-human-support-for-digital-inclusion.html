Using targeted human support to enable digital inclusion

The Roxton Practice offers multiple ways to access digital therapeutics (DTx), recognising that one digital route will not suit everyone.

The practice learned that tablets fixed to waiting room walls were ineffective — people who needed help stayed away. What did work was combining technology with human-to-human support.

People can use their own phone or computer to access care and share their data. Or they can use a community computer or a device at a GP surgery, library, pharmacy or workplace, with someone on hand to help. People move between these environments as their needs change.

The practice places trained helpers and volunteer support workers (sometimes referred to as Care Connectors or care navigators) in locations patients already visit. They can help patients set up the NHS App, which they can use to track their condition and submit readings.

The practice has found that employing non-clinical staff to assist people with technology in the waiting areas has huge benefits. The practice is now considering how to reorganise its lobby areas to offer more rooms for private one-to-one digital support.
