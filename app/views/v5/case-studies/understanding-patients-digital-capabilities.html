Find ways of understanding patients' digital capabilities

Before suggesting any digital therapeutics (DTx), the Roxton Practice assesses whether each patient can use one. They have built this assessment into the information they gather about their patients.

The practice asked all 12,500 of its patients about their confidence in and preferences for digital care. The practice communicated in several ways: face-to-face, by phone, letter, text and online, ensuring it did not miss people who may be less digitally savvy.

Each patient is then assigned a digital readiness level, which is reflected in their health record, and staff use it to decide how to reach that person. A digitally confident person might be sent a link. Someone less so might be offered a printed form to complete, or face-to-face help.

All information obtained is incorporated into the NHS Electronic Patient Record (EPR), and clinicians and practice staff are prompted to keep it up to date.
