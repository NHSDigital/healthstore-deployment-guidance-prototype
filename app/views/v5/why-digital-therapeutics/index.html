Why NHS England is an advocate of Digital Therapeutics (DTx)

The NHS views software apps (sometimes referred to as Digital Therapeutics or DTx) used to treat, manage, or prevent deterioration as an effective way to improve care. The NHS HealthStore provides patients with safe, NICE-approved digital tools.

The NHS believes DTx best supports people by:

  • enabling shorter wait times due to software-based treatments expanding access to care without needing a physical doctor's appointment
  • making treatment and support more accessible
  • reducing patient hospital visits by enabling doctors to monitor conditions from afar, reducing patient travel and preventing conditions from getting worse
  • expanding ways of reaching high-quality care — high-quality treatment, via an app
  • enabling doctors to reevaluate how they use their time, ensuring all patients receive the best care, in the most appropriate way

Data generated by DTx is strictly governed to prioritise patient safety, privacy, and clinical effectiveness. It is primarily used to tailor direct care, monitor patient progress, and evaluate treatment outcomes.
