How digital therapeutics (DTx) improve patient outcomes

Digital therapeutics (DTx) are clinically-proven software apps used to prevent, manage, or treat medical conditions. They improve patient outcomes by delivering tailored behavioural therapies, tracking symptoms, and increasing treatment adherence.

Digital therapeutics can improve patient outcomes in several ways, such as:

Supporting behaviour changes over time

Sustained behaviour change in exercise, diet, medication adherence and symptom monitoring is difficult to achieve during short appointments. Digital therapeutics provide daily support that can enable lasting change.

Building confidence to self-manage

Providing NICE-approved digital therapy that helps patients understand what is safe to do can help them engage with their recovery. A digital therapeutic provides structure and reassurance that helps build confidence in managing their health. Feeling able to manage their own health can help reduce anxiety-driven contacts and unnecessary appointments.

Extending the reach of a clinical programme

For patients who cannot access in-person services due to their location, or work or caring responsibilities, a digital therapeutic may be the only structured help available. For patients who have completed a clinical programme, it provides an opportunity for continued support.

Reducing pressure on services

Evidence from deployed digital therapeutics shows that people who engage with structured, digital support reduce the likelihood of unplanned GP appointments, emergency department attendances and hospital readmissions.
