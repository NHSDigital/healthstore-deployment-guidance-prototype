How to introduce digital therapeutics (DTx) to your patients

Language to use

One of the most important variables in whether a patient engages with a digital therapeutic (DTx) is the clinical conversation at the point of referral.

Patients who understand from their clinician why they have been recommended a DTx, are significantly more likely to onboard and continue using it.

Suggested language to use during brief conversations that introduce DTx:

Suggested language when introducing digital therapeutics
Say this Avoid this
"I'm recommending this as part of your treatment" "There's an app you might want to try"
"This has been prescribed for your recovery" "You could have a look at this if you want"
"This is part of your care — it's free and approved by the NHS" "It's just a digital thing, give it a go"

During the next clinical contact, ask how the patient is getting on with the app.

Digital therapeutics as an integral part of treatment

We know that some people can be hostile towards DTxs. One clinician talked about them 'going against the [belief] that in-person help is best'.

Some people may think they are being 'fobbed off' by being given an app. Research shows that making it clear that DTx is part of their treatment can help reduce negative associations with the app.
