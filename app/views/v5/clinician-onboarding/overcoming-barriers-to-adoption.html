Overcoming barriers to using DTx

Refer to Case studies and best practice for more examples of best practice.

Assessing a patients' digital capabilities

By assessing a patient's digital capabilities as part of routine clinical contact, practitioners can increase the likelihood of the care pathway being well matched to the patient's digital capabilities.

At the Roxton Practice in North Lincolnshire, they implemented a formal scoring system showing patients' digital capabilities.

This was then incorporated into pathways that would define the type of communications each patient would receive.

Using in-person help to support DTx adoption

Systematic and targeted in-person help can support people in onboarding to apps. This help can be provided by people with limited clinical training.

Supporting patients in using the NHS App

Research shows that while many people may struggle to install an app, if they are helped to do so, they will use it once it's installed.

Some clinical settings have successfully used non-clinical 'digital navigators' to help onboard people.

There are several resources available to help people install the NHS App:

  • NHS App step by step guide
  • Setting up the NHS App

Working with local partners and third-sector organisations

Many practices that have successfully helped people onboard The NHS App and DTx have worked with other organisations within their neighbourhood or local area. Taking this multi-agency approach can help address complex health and care needs.
